Multi-Radar Inertial Odometry using mmWave Imaging Radar

Jui-Te Huang, Ruoyang Xu, Akshay Hinduja, Michael Kaess
Carnegie Mellon University
arXiv Video

Abstract

In the world of robotics, state estimation is a crucial task, relying on sensors like cameras, LiDAR, and IMUs. However, challenging environments, such as those with adverse weather conditions and low-light scenarios, often degrade these sensors' performance. The emerging 4D imaging radar technology is capable of providing robust perception in adverse conditions. Despite its potential, several challenges remain, particularly in indoor settings with limited apparent geometric features. Moreover, disparities in radar data resolution and field of view can lead to inaccurate measurements. While prior research has explored radar-inertial odometry based on Doppler velocity information, challenges remain for the estimation of 3D motion because of the discrepancy in the field of view and resolution of the radar sensor. In this paper, we address Doppler velocity measurement uncertainties. We present a method to optimize body frame velocity while managing Doppler velocity uncertainty. Based on our observations, we propose a dual imaging radar configuration to mitigate the challenge of discrepancy in radar data. To attain precise 3D state estimation, we introduce a fixed-lag smoother optimization strategy that seamlessly integrates radar data with a consumer-grade IMU sensor. Finally, we evaluate our approach using real-world 3D motion data.
